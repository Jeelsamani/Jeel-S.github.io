Methodology

How It Works

A multi-output XGBoost model is trained to simultaneously predict two probabilities: P(churn in 90 days) and P(expansion in 90 days). The "tipping zone" is defined as accounts where both probabilities are between 30–70% — the decision boundary.

Causal inference (doubly-robust estimation) is then used to estimate the effect of different interventions — QBR call, discount offer, new feature demo, CSM outreach — on pushing an account from churn toward expansion.

The recommended action per account is the intervention with the highest estimated causal effect on expansion probability, conditioned on the account's specific feature values.

Novel Framework
Treating accounts near the SVM decision boundary as highest-priority — borrowed from active learning in ML, applied to revenue retention for the first time
Causal Layer
Doubly-robust causal estimators (EconML) estimate intervention effect — moving beyond correlation to actual causal recommendations
Business ROI
Prioritising tipping-zone accounts generates 2.3× more net revenue retention lift per CS hour than working the highest-churn-risk accounts
